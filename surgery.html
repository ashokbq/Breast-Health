Surgery

Menu

In most patients, surgery is the treatment option followed in order to remove the cancer from the breast. Surgery may be of two types:

  • it may involve removal of the whole breast that has cancer
  • it may involve removal of the cancer, but not the breast itself (breast-conserving surgery).

Chemotherapy

Menu

Chemotherapy involves the use of drugs which help to stop the growth of cancer cells, either by killing the cells or by stopping them from dividing. The drugs may be taken orally or by injection (systemic chemotherapy), or it may be placed directly into the cerebrospinal fluid, an organ, or a body cavity such as the abdomen in order to affect cancer cells in those areas (regional chemotherapy).

Targeted Therapy

Menu

In targeted therapy, drugs or other substances are used to identify and attach to specific cancer cells, without harming the normal cells. Once attached to cancer cells, they may kill them, block their growth or prevent them from spreading. Monoclonal antibodies and tyrosine kinase inhibitors are two types of targeted therapies used in the treatment of breast cancer.
