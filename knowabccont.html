Interesting Breast Facts

What is Breast cancer?

Breast cancer is a form of cancer that affects the cells of the breast.

In breast cancer, cells in the breast become abnormal and multiply without control, leading to formation of a tumor.


Worldwide Breast cancer Statistics

Breast cancer is the most common form of cancer seen in women worldwide. It comprises 16% of all cancers in females.

According to the WHO Global Burden of Disease, 2004, 69% of all breast cancer deaths occur in developing countries.

By 2020, breast cancer cases are expected to increase by 26%. Most of these cases will be seen in developing countries.

Breast cancer in India

The incidence of breast cancer is increasing in India as women adopt western lifestyles of marrying later and having children later in life.

It is estimated that over 100,000 new breast cancer patients are diagnosed annually in India.



The incidence of this disease has been consistently increasing at a rate of 0.5-2% every year.

This rise is being seen across all regions of India and in all age groups but especially in the younger age groups (<45 years).

In India, more than 80% of breast cancer patients are younger than 60 years of age, with a significant proportion of Indian breast cancer patients being younger than 35 years of age.

Signs and Symptoms

The presence of a lump or mass in or near the breast

Swelling of the breast, or a lump or swelling under the arm

Irritation or dimpling of skin in the breast region.

Redness, scaliness, or thickening of the nipple or breast skin.

Breast or nipple pain

Nipple retraction (turning inward)

Nipple discharge

Treatment options

Surgery:In most patients, surgery is the treatment option followed in order to remove the cancer from the breast.

Surgery may be of two types:

  • it may involve removal of the whole breast that has cancer
  • it may involve removal of the cancer, but not the breast itself (breast-conserving surgery).

Radiation therapy:High-energy x-rays or other types of radiation are used to kill cancer cells or prevent them from growing further.

Chemotherapy:Chemotherapy involves the use of drugs which help to stop the growth of cancer cells, either by killing the cells or by stopping them from dividing.

The drug therapy may be systemic - taken orally or by injection

or

it may be regional - placed directly into the cerebrospinal fluid, an organ, or a body cavity such as the abdomen in order to affect cancer cells in those areas.

Hormone Therapy: Hormone therapy involves removal of hormones or blocking of their activity so that cancer cells are unable to grow.

Targeted therapy: In targeted therapy, drugs or other substances are used to identify and attach to specific cancer cells, without harming the normal cells.

Once attached to cancer cells, they may kill them, block their growth or prevent them from spreading.

Monoclonal antibodies and tyrosine kinase inhibitors are two types of targeted therapies used in the treatment of breast cancer.

Tips for cancer survivors

Regular follow-up care -

every 3 to 4 months with your doctor during the first 2 to 3 years after treatment

once or twice a year after that.

Live a healthy life -

Quit smoking

maintain a healthy weight

Make healthy food choices after your treatment -

  • Control your calories to achieve a healthy weight
  • Eat more fruits and vegetables
  • Eat more plant-based foods
  • Eat more fibre
  • Eat less fat
  • Eat less sugar

Develop a wellness plan to take care of your physical, emotional, social, and spiritual needs.

Physical activity can be empowering and can enhance your overall well-being.

Be part of a support group to share your
feelings and experiences with others.
